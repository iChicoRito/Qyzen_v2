================================================================================
                    STUDENT ONBOARDING & USAGE GUIDE
                         Qyzen Learning Platform
================================================================================

Welcome to Qyzen! This guide will walk you through every step of getting started
and using the platform, from the initial registration process all the way to
taking assessments and exploring your learning resources.

If you have any questions not answered in this guide, contact your instructor
or your school's IT support.

================================================================================
PHASE 1: INITIAL REGISTRATION VIA GOOGLE FORM
================================================================================

STEP 1: Access the Google Form
-------------------------------
Your instructor will provide you with a link to a Google Form. This link may be
shared through:
  • Email
  • Your school's learning management system
  • A message or announcement in class
  • A printed handout

Copy the link into your web browser or click it directly.

STEP 2: Complete the Google Form
-------------------------------
The Google Form will ask you to provide the following information:

  REQUIRED FIELDS:

  • Full Name (as you wish it to appear in the system)
  • Email Address (use an email you check regularly - this is critical!)
  • Student ID or Enrollment Number (provided by your school)
  • Grade or Class Section (your current class or section)
  • Confirm Your Email Address (re-enter your email to avoid typos)

IMPORTANT: Double-check your email address. The system will send your login
credentials to this email. If you provide the wrong email, you will not receive
your registration link and will need to contact your instructor to correct it.

STEP 3: Submit the Form
-------------------------------
After filling out all required fields, click the "Submit" button at the bottom
of the form.

You will see a confirmation message. The form has been successfully submitted.

YOUR INSTRUCTOR'S ROLE:
After your instructor collects all student responses, they will use the Qyzen
administrative interface to process the batch and generate credentials for each
student. This typically happens within 1-2 business days.

================================================================================
PHASE 2: RECEIVING YOUR CREDENTIALS
================================================================================

STEP 1: Watch for the Registration Email
-------------------------------
After your instructor processes the batch, you will receive an automated email
from the Qyzen system. This email will arrive at the email address you provided
in the Google Form.

Email details:
  FROM: system@qyzen-platform.local or noreply@your-school-domain
  SUBJECT: "Welcome to Qyzen - Complete Your Registration"

STEP 2: Examine the Registration Email
-------------------------------
The email will contain:

  1. A PERSONALIZED GREETING
     Example: "Hello [Your Name],"

  2. A WELCOME MESSAGE
     This explains that an account has been created for you on the Qyzen
     platform.

  3. YOUR TEMPORARY USERNAME
     The email will show a temporary username. You will use this to log in
     after you complete account setup.
     Example: The temporary username might be something like "student.2024.001"

  4. A ONE-TIME REGISTRATION LINK
     This is a clickable button or link labeled "Complete Your Account Setup"
     or "Set Your Password."

     IMPORTANT: This link is unique to you and expires after a certain period
     (typically 24-72 hours). Do not share this link with anyone.

  5. EXPIRATION WARNING
     The email states that this link will expire. If it expires, contact your
     instructor to request a new registration link.

  6. INSTRUCTIONS FOR THE NEXT STEP
     The email explains that you need to click the link to create your password.

STEP 3: What If You Don't Receive the Email?
-------------------------------
If you don't receive the registration email within 2-3 days:

  1. Check your Spam or Junk folder - it may have been filtered there
  2. Check that you used the correct email address in the Google Form
  3. Contact your instructor and provide your full name and the email you used
     Your instructor can resend the registration link or verify your entry

================================================================================
PHASE 3: ACCOUNT ACTIVATION & FIRST LOGIN
================================================================================

STEP 1: Click the Registration Link
-------------------------------
Open the registration email on the same device where you plan to use Qyzen
(phone, tablet, or computer - you can use any of these).

Click the "Complete Your Account Setup" or similar button/link in the email.

This will take you to the Qyzen system in a new browser window or tab.

STEP 2: Set Your Permanent Password
-------------------------------
You will now see the account creation page. The page will display:

  • Your temporary username (read-only, you cannot change it yet)
  • A "Password" field where you enter your new password
  • A "Confirm Password" field where you re-enter your password
  • A "Create Account" button

PASSWORD REQUIREMENTS:
Your password must be at least 8 characters long and should include:
  ✓ At least one uppercase letter (A-Z)
  ✓ At least one lowercase letter (a-z)
  ✓ At least one number (0-9)
  ✓ At least one special character (!@#$%^&*)

STRONG PASSWORD EXAMPLE:
  Welcome@Qyzen2024

WEAK PASSWORD EXAMPLE:
  password123  (no uppercase, no special character)

SECURITY TIPS:
  • Do not use your name, username, or school ID in your password
  • Do not use easily guessable information (birthdate, phone number, etc.)
  • Use a password you haven't used on other websites
  • Write it down somewhere safe until you memorize it

STEP 3: Confirm Your Profile Information
-------------------------------
After you set your password, you may be prompted to confirm or review your
profile information:

  • Full Name (should match the Google Form)
  • Email Address (should match what you provided in the Google Form)
  • Student ID (should match the Google Form)
  • Grade/Class Section (should match the Google Form)

Review this information carefully. If any detail is incorrect:
  • Contact your instructor to request a correction
  • Do not proceed if critical information (like email) is wrong

If everything is correct, proceed to the next step.

STEP 4: Complete Account Creation
-------------------------------
Click the "Create Account" or "Complete Setup" button.

The system will confirm that your account has been successfully created. You
may see a message like:

  "Welcome, [Your Name]! Your account is now active. Redirecting to login..."

The page will automatically take you to the login page, or you may see a button
to proceed to login.

STEP 5: Log In for the First Time
-------------------------------
On the login page, you will see two fields:

  Username: [enter the temporary username from the email]
  Password: [enter the password you just created]

Example login:
  Username: student.2024.001
  Password: Welcome@Qyzen2024

Click "Log In" or press Enter.

STEP 6: Access Your Dashboard
-------------------------------
After a successful login, you will see your Qyzen Dashboard. This is your
personal "home page" in the system.

The dashboard typically displays:
  • Your name and profile picture (if set)
  • A navigation menu on the left or top
  • Recent announcements or notifications
  • Quick links to your assessments, grades, and profile
  • A summary of any pending work

Congratulations! You are now logged into Qyzen.

================================================================================
PHASE 4: NAVIGATING THE SYSTEM & USING CORE FEATURES
================================================================================

SECTION A: DASHBOARD OVERVIEW
-------------------------------
Your dashboard is the central hub of Qyzen. When you first log in, you will
see:

  • Welcome message with your name
  • Navigation menu (left sidebar or top menu bar)
  • Notification alerts (if any)
  • Quick access buttons or tiles for key features

SECTION B: NAVIGATION MENU
-------------------------------
The main navigation menu includes the following sections:

  1. DASHBOARD
     Return to your home page at any time. Shows your overview and recent
     activity.

  2. ASSESSMENTS (or QUIZZES)
     Where you access all the quizzes and tests assigned to you. This is
     where you will spend most of your time.

  3. GRADEBOOK
     View your grades and performance on all completed assessments. See
     scores, feedback from your instructor, and a history of your work.

  4. ANNOUNCEMENTS
     Read important messages and announcements from your instructors or school.

  5. RESOURCES (or LEARNING MATERIALS)
     Access study materials, documents, videos, or other resources your
     instructor has shared.

  6. MY ACCOUNT (or PROFILE)
     Manage your personal information, change your password, update your
     profile picture, or adjust notification preferences.

  7. HELP or SUPPORT
     Links to FAQs, contact information, or a help section.

SECTION C: TAKING AN ASSESSMENT
-------------------------------
This is one of the most important features. Follow these steps:

STEP 1: Navigate to Assessments
  From the navigation menu, click "Assessments" or "Quizzes"

STEP 2: View Your Assigned Assessments
  You will see a list of all assessments assigned to your class or section.
  Each assessment will show:

    • Title (name of the quiz or test)
    • Subject or Course
    • Status (e.g., "Not Started", "In Progress", "Submitted", "Graded")
    • Start Date and Due Date
    • A button to "Start", "Continue", or "View Results"

  The color of the assessment tile may indicate its status:
    • Green: Ready to take / recently graded
    • Orange/Yellow: Due soon / in progress
    • Red: Overdue / not started
    • Gray: Not yet available / archived

STEP 3: Select an Assessment
  Click on the assessment you want to take. This will open a detail page that
  shows:

    • Full title and description
    • Instructions or special notes from your instructor
    • Number of questions
    • Time limit (if applicable)
    • Passing score (usually 70%)
    • Start button (or Continue button if you began it before)

  Read all instructions carefully before starting.

STEP 4: Start the Assessment
  Click the "Start Assessment", "Begin Quiz", or "Launch" button.

  You will enter the assessment interface.

STEP 5: Understand the Assessment Interface
  While taking the assessment, you will see:

    • Current question number and total questions (e.g., "Question 3 of 20")
    • The question text or prompt
    • Answer options (multiple choice, true/false, short answer, or other type)
    • A "Next" or "Continue" button to move to the next question
    • A "Previous" button to review earlier questions (if allowed)
    • A timer showing remaining time (if time-limited)
    • A progress bar showing how many questions you've answered
    • A "Save and Exit" or "Submit Assessment" button

  IMPORTANT: Different assessments may have different rules:
    • Some allow you to go back and change answers
    • Some do not allow you to review after you answer
    • Some are time-limited; if time runs out, they auto-submit
    • Some must be completed in one session; you cannot save and return later
    • Some allow multiple attempts

  Your instructor will specify these rules in the assessment instructions.

STEP 6: Answer the Questions
  For each question:

    MULTIPLE CHOICE:
      Click the radio button or checkbox next to your chosen answer, then
      proceed.

    TRUE/FALSE:
      Click "True" or "False", then proceed.

    SHORT ANSWER or ESSAY:
      Click in the text box and type your answer. Use as much space as
      needed.

    OTHER FORMATS:
      Follow the on-screen instructions for matching, drag-and-drop, or
      other question types.

  Take your time. Read each question carefully and select your best answer.

STEP 7: Review Before Submitting (If Allowed)
  Before you submit, you have an opportunity to review your answers.

  Click "Review" or look for a review button. You will see a summary showing:

    • All questions you answered
    • All questions you skipped
    • The answer you selected for each question (or indication that you
      skipped it)

  If you want to change an answer:
    1. Click on the question number in the list
    2. The interface will return to that question
    3. Change your answer
    4. Return to the review screen

  SKIPPED QUESTIONS:
  If you see any questions you skipped, you have a chance to answer them now.

STEP 8: Submit the Assessment
  Once you are satisfied with your answers, click the "Submit Assessment" or
  "Finish Quiz" button.

  A confirmation message will appear:
    "Are you sure you want to submit? You will not be able to change your
     answers after submission."

  Click "Yes, Submit" or "Confirm" to complete the assessment.

STEP 9: View Your Submission Confirmation
  After submission, you will see:

    • A confirmation message: "Your assessment has been submitted successfully"
    • Your score (if the assessment is auto-graded immediately)
    • A timestamp showing when you submitted
    • A button to return to the assessment list or dashboard

  Your assessment is now submitted. Your instructor will review it and may add
  feedback or a grade.

STEP 10: Check Your Grade and Feedback
  After your instructor grades the assessment, you can view:

    • Your final score
    • Your performance on each question
    • Written feedback from your instructor
    • Whether you passed or need to retake it (if retakes are allowed)

  To view this, go to:
    1. Assessments > [Assessment Name] > View Results
    2. OR Gradebook > [Assessment Name]

SECTION D: VIEWING YOUR GRADEBOOK
-------------------------------
Your Gradebook shows your performance across all assessments.

STEP 1: Access the Gradebook
  From the navigation menu, click "Gradebook" or "Grades"

STEP 2: Understand the Gradebook Layout
  You will see a table or list showing:

    • Assessment Name/Title
    • Date You Submitted (or Date Due)
    • Your Score (e.g., 85/100 or 85%)
    • Status (Graded, Pending, etc.)
    • Any comments or feedback from your instructor
    • Your letter grade (A, B, C, etc.) if applicable

STEP 3: Filter or Sort (If Available)
  Many gradebooks let you:
    • Sort by date, score, or status
    • Filter by subject or time period
    • View only graded or pending items

  Use these options to find specific assessments.

STEP 4: View Detailed Feedback
  Click on an assessment in the gradebook to see:
    • Detailed breakdown of your performance
    • Your answer and the correct answer (for auto-graded questions)
    • Written feedback from your instructor
    • Opportunities to retake (if allowed)

SECTION E: READING ANNOUNCEMENTS
-------------------------------
Your instructor uses announcements to communicate important information.

STEP 1: Access Announcements
  From the navigation menu, click "Announcements" or "Messages"

STEP 2: Read Active Announcements
  You will see a list of announcements, newest first. Each shows:

    • Title
    • Date posted
    • Preview of the message
    • A "Read More" option if it's long

STEP 3: Stay Informed
  Check announcements regularly (at least 2-3 times per week) to stay aware of:
    • Upcoming assessment dates
    • Changes to schedules or assignments
    • New resources or materials
    • Important reminders from your instructor

SECTION F: ACCESSING LEARNING RESOURCES
-------------------------------
Your instructor may provide study materials, documents, videos, or other
resources.

STEP 1: Access Resources
  From the navigation menu, click "Resources", "Materials", or "Learning Content"

STEP 2: Browse Available Resources
  You will see a list or folder structure of resources organized by:
    • Subject
    • Topic
    • Week or unit
    • Resource type (document, video, link, etc.)

STEP 3: Download or View
  • For documents (PDF, Word): Click to download or view in your browser
  • For videos: Click to watch or stream
  • For links: Click to open the external resource in a new tab

SECTION G: MANAGING YOUR ACCOUNT & PROFILE
-------------------------------
STEP 1: Access My Account
  From the navigation menu, click "My Account", "Profile", or your name

STEP 2: Update Your Profile
  You can update:

    • Profile Picture: Click "Upload Photo" to add a picture of yourself
    • Display Name: Change how your name appears (if allowed)
    • Bio or About: Add a short description of yourself
    • Contact Information: Update phone number or secondary email (if available)

STEP 3: Change Your Password
  To change your password (recommended every 3-6 months):

    1. Click "Change Password" or "Security Settings"
    2. Enter your current password
    3. Enter your new password
    4. Confirm the new password
    5. Click "Update" or "Save"

  Use the same password requirements as before:
    • At least 8 characters
    • Mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters
    • Not based on personal information

STEP 4: Notification Preferences (If Available)
  You may be able to control how you receive notifications:

    • Email notifications on/off
    • Frequency (real-time, daily digest, weekly)
    • Types of notifications (grades, announcements, reminders)

  Set these according to your preference.

STEP 5: Review and Exit
  After making changes, click "Save" or "Update". You will see a confirmation
  message that your changes have been saved.

TROUBLESHOOTING & FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
================================================================================

Q: I forgot my password. What do I do?
A: On the login page, look for a link that says "Forgot Your Password?" or
   "Forgot Password?" Click it. You will be asked to enter your email address.
   The system will send you a link to reset your password. Follow the
   instructions in that email.

Q: I didn't receive the registration email. What should I do?
A: 1. Check your Spam or Junk folder.
   2. Check that the email address you entered in the Google Form was correct.
   3. Wait 24-48 hours (the instructor may still be processing registrations).
   4. Contact your instructor with your full name and email address.

Q: I clicked the registration link, but it says "Link Expired." What now?
A: The registration link is only valid for 24-72 hours. If it expired:
   1. Contact your instructor
   2. Provide your full name and email address
   3. Your instructor can resend the registration link or create a new one

Q: I'm logged in but can't find an assessment I'm supposed to take.
A: 1. Check the assessment list - it may not be visible yet if the instructor
      hasn't released it.
   2. Check the start date and due date. If today is before the start date,
      you cannot take it yet.
   3. Ask your instructor if the assessment has been assigned to your class
      or section.

Q: I started an assessment but didn't finish it. Can I continue later?
A: It depends on the assessment settings. Some assessments allow you to save
   and return later; others must be completed in one session.
   1. Go back to the assessments list
   2. If the assessment shows "In Progress" or "Continue", click that button
      to resume
   3. If it shows "Submitted" or "Graded", you've already finished
   4. If you're unsure, ask your instructor

Q: I submitted an assessment by mistake. Can I re-submit?
A: That depends on whether your instructor allows multiple attempts.
   1. Check your gradebook or the assessment page for "Retake" or "Attempt 2"
      button
   2. If you see a retake option, you can submit again
   3. If there's no retake option, contact your instructor to ask if they
      can allow you to resubmit

Q: My score is showing as "Pending". When will it be graded?
A: "Pending" means your instructor hasn't graded it yet.
   1. Multiple-choice questions may be graded automatically and show a score
      immediately.
   2. Essay or short-answer questions must be graded by your instructor.
   3. Check back in a few days. Your instructor should grade submissions
      within 3-5 business days.
   4. If it's been longer than a week, send your instructor a polite reminder.

Q: I can't log in. It says my username or password is incorrect.
A: 1. Double-check that you're using the correct temporary username from the
      registration email.
   2. Make sure Caps Lock is not on. Passwords are case-sensitive.
   3. Re-enter your password slowly and carefully.
   4. If you still can't log in, use "Forgot Your Password?" to reset it.
   5. If that doesn't work, contact your instructor or IT support.

Q: The website is showing an error or not loading properly.
A: 1. Refresh your browser (press F5 or Ctrl+R)
   2. Clear your browser's cache and cookies
   3. Try a different browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge)
   4. Try on a different device (phone, tablet, computer)
   5. Check that you have a good internet connection
   6. If the problem persists, contact your instructor or IT support

Q: Can I use Qyzen on my phone or tablet?
A: Yes! Qyzen is designed to work on phones, tablets, and computers. However:
   1. Some assessments may be easier on a larger screen (computer)
   2. Your instructor may restrict certain features on mobile devices
   3. Make sure you have a stable internet connection during the assessment
   4. Don't close the browser or switch apps during a timed assessment

Q: What should I do if I lose internet connection during an assessment?
A: 1. Try to reconnect as quickly as possible
   2. If your connection returns, you may be able to resume the assessment
   3. If you cannot reconnect, contact your instructor as soon as possible
      and explain what happened
   4. Your instructor may allow you to retake the assessment

Q: How long will I have access to my account?
A: You will have access to your account for the duration of the course or
   school year. After the course ends, your instructor or school will
   communicate whether your access continues or expires.

Q: Can I print the assessment or take it offline?
A: Most assessments must be taken online. However, your instructor may allow:
   1. Printing the assessment questions (if it's not time-limited)
   2. Downloading study materials
   3. For specific accommodations (if you have a documented disability)

   Ask your instructor if you need to take the assessment in a different format.

Q: Who should I contact if I have more questions?
A: In order of recommendation:
   1. Your instructor (they can answer specific questions about assignments)
   2. Your school's IT support or help desk
   3. Check the Qyzen Help section within the platform
   4. Your school's student support services

FINAL TIPS FOR SUCCESS
================================================================================

1. PREPARE FOR ASSESSMENTS
   • Review the study materials your instructor provides
   • Ask questions about topics you don't understand
   • Take practice quizzes if available
   • Get a good night's sleep before the assessment

2. MANAGE YOUR TIME
   • Check the due dates for all assessments
   • Start early rather than waiting until the last minute
   • Note any time limits on individual assessments
   • Plan to complete assessments when you're alert and focused

3. FOLLOW INSTRUCTIONS CAREFULLY
   • Read all assessment instructions before starting
   • Pay attention to special notes from your instructor
   • Understand what format answers should be in
   • Confirm any special requirements (e.g., essay length, allowed resources)

4. STAY CONNECTED WITH YOUR CLASS
   • Check announcements regularly
   • Monitor your email for system notifications
   • Ask questions when you don't understand something
   • Communicate with your instructor about any issues

5. MAINTAIN ACADEMIC INTEGRITY
   • Complete all assessments honestly and independently
   • Do not share assessment questions or answers with other students
   • Do not use unauthorized materials or aids (unless your instructor allows)
   • Follow the rules your instructor has set for each assessment

6. KEEP YOUR ACCOUNT SECURE
   • Never share your username or password with anyone
   • Don't log in from unsecured public WiFi for sensitive assessments
   • Log out when you're finished, especially on shared computers
   • Report any suspicious activity to your instructor or IT support

7. SAVE YOUR WORK
   • On essay or short-answer questions, consider typing in a text editor
      first and pasting into the system
   • This protects you against accidentally losing your work if there's a
      connection issue

8. REACH OUT EARLY IF YOU HAVE PROBLEMS
   • Don't wait until the last day to report technical issues
   • Contact your instructor or IT support as soon as something goes wrong
   • Provide details about what happened and when

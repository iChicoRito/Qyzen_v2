# Profile Page Walkthrough

A plain-language tour of the profile page — what you see and what you can do.
The page is the same for every user (admin, educator, student), except that
students can only change their email and photos, not their name.

---

## Getting to the page

Look at the bottom of the menu bar on the left side of the screen. There you
see your small round photo, your name, and your role. Click it. A short menu
opens. Choose **Profile settings**. This takes you to your profile page.

---

## What the page looks like

At the top it says **Account Settings**, with the line *"Manage your account
settings and preferences."* underneath.

Below that are two tabs you can switch between:

- **Personal Information** — your photos, name, and email (this one is open first).
- **Security** — where you change your password.

### The Personal Information tab

This tab has a card called **Profile Media** ("Update your profile picture and
cover photo"), which shows:

- A wide **cover photo** banner across the top. If you haven't chosen one, it
  says *"No cover photo selected."*
- Your round **profile picture** sitting near the bottom-left of the banner. If
  you haven't added a photo, it shows your initials instead (for example "JD").

Just under the photos you see your **first and last name**, a small badge with
your user ID (like "U-12345"), and your current **email address**.

Further down are your editable fields:

- **Given Name** — your first name.
- **Last Name** — your surname.
- **Email Address** — shown in a grey box you can't type into, with a
  **Change Email Address** button beside it.

> If you are a student, the Given Name and Last Name boxes are greyed out and a
> note reads *"Students can update email and media only."* You can still change
> your photos and email.

---

## Changing your profile picture

1. Move your mouse over your profile picture — a small camera icon appears.
   Click the picture.
2. A window opens for you to pick a picture from your device. You can choose a
   PNG, JPG, or WebP image up to 2 MB. (If you pick something too big or not an
   image, a small message tells you: *"Image size must be 2 MB or less"* or
   *"Please choose an image file."*)
3. A **cropping window** opens, titled *"Crop Profile Picture."* You see your
   image inside a fixed square frame.
   - **Drag** the image to line up the part you want inside the square.
   - Use the **Zoom** slider to make the image bigger or smaller (from 1x up to
     3x).
4. When it looks right, click **Apply**. To back out, click **Cancel**.
5. The cropped photo now shows as your new picture, ready to be saved.

To save it for good, scroll down and click **Save Changes**. A message confirms
*"Profile settings updated successfully."*

---

## Changing your cover photo

1. Move your mouse over the wide banner at the top — it shows *"Edit cover
   photo."* Click it.
2. Pick a picture from your device (PNG, JPG, or WebP, up to 2 MB). The same
   size and file-type rules apply.
3. Unlike the profile picture, there is **no cropping step** — the picture you
   choose is used as-is and appears right away in the banner.
4. Scroll down and click **Save Changes** to keep it. You'll see
   *"Profile settings updated successfully."*

---

## Changing your password

1. Click the **Security** tab.
2. You see a note: *"Update your password. It must be at least 8 characters and
   include uppercase, lowercase, a number, and a special character."*
3. Fill in the two boxes:
   - **New Password**
   - **Confirm Password** (type the same thing again)
4. Click **Save Changes**.

If everything is fine, you'll see *"Password updated successfully."*

If something is off, a short note appears under the box telling you what to fix,
for example:

- *"Password must be at least 8 characters."*
- *"Password must include at least one uppercase letter."*
- *"Password must include at least one number."*
- *"Passwords do not match."* (the two boxes don't match)

> Note: this form asks only for a **new** password and a confirmation — it does
> not ask for your current password.

---

## Changing your name

1. On the **Personal Information** tab, click into the **Given Name** or
   **Last Name** box and type the new value. (Students cannot do this.)
2. Click **Save Changes**. The page confirms *"Profile settings updated
   successfully."*

---

## Changing your email address

Your email is the address you sign in with, so it is changed through your Google
account rather than by typing.

1. On the **Personal Information** tab, click **Change Email Address**.
2. A small window opens titled *"Change email address"* explaining: *"You'll
   pick a Google account on the next screen. Your sign-in email will be changed
   to that account's email, and your current email will be replaced."*
3. Click **Continue** (or **Cancel** to stop).
4. You are taken to Google to choose an account. Pick the Google account whose
   email you want to use.
5. You come back to the profile page and see a message telling you what
   happened:
   - Success: *"This Google email is now your sign-in email."*
   - If that email belongs to someone else: *"That Google email is already used
     by another account."*
   - If it's already your email: *"That Google account is already your email.
     Pick a different one to switch."*

---

## Layout and appearance

Changing the light/dark look and theme is **not** done on the profile page. That
option lives on your main **dashboard** page, opened from a button there. Any
theme you pick applies everywhere, including this profile page, and is
remembered the next time you sign in.

---

## Quick summary of the buttons

| Where | What it does |
|-------|--------------|
| Profile picture (camera icon) | Pick and crop a new profile photo |
| Cover banner ("Edit cover photo") | Pick a new cover photo (no cropping) |
| **Change Email Address** | Switch your sign-in email using a Google account |
| **Save Changes** | Save your photos, name, or new password |
| **Personal Information** / **Security** tabs | Switch between details and password |

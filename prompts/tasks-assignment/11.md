# Objective
## Admin Resend Credentials with Email Correction

---

## Role
You are a backend and admin-panel developer responsible for student account management workflows.

---

## Description
The admin panel currently allows editing a student's email address and resending a verification message. When an email contains a typo and is corrected by the admin, the resend action delivers only a verification link without including the student’s login credentials. Students who receive only the verification email are therefore unable to sign in because they lack their password or initial credential set. The required behavior is that every time the admin triggers a resend to a given email — regardless of whether the email was corrected or not — the system must include the student’s credentials in the message. Before implementing this change, the existing code paths, email-trigger logic, and any related configuration must be audited for regressions, security concerns, or unintended dependencies that could break other functionality.

---

## Primary Objective
Ensure that any admin-initiated resend action sends both a verification link and the student’s current credentials, independent of prior typo corrections.

---

## Secondary Objectives
- Allow admins to edit a student’s email address when a typo is identified.
- Make the resend action consistently deliver credentials so students never lack login information.
- Audit the existing email sending, credential generation, and admin workflows for red flags or dependencies before applying the fix.

---

## Success Criteria
- After an admin edits a student’s email and clicks resend, the message includes the student’s credentials.
- The resend action always includes credentials, even when no email correction was made.
- No existing functionality (registration, password reset, manual credential views) is broken by the change.

---

## Constraints
- The fix must apply only to the admin-side resend workflow.
- The audit must be completed before any code change is implemented.

---

## Context & Dependencies
- A student creation or import process generates credentials and optionally sends them to the student’s email.
- The current resend function is scoped only to verification and does not reference credential payloads.
- There is a distinct verification flow that may share templates or methods with the resend action.
- Editing the email field on the admin panel may or may not trigger side effects on related account records.

---

## Stated Assumptions
- The student record always has credentials stored or generatable at the time of resend.

---

## Stakeholders
- Admin users responsible for managing student accounts and correcting typos.
- Students who rely on receiving both verification and credential information.

---

## Supporting Tasks

### Audit & Analysis
- Trace the admin resend action through controller, service, and email layers to identify where the credential payload is dropped.
- Check whether the email template used for resend supports a credentials variable.
- Review any conditions or feature flags that gate credential inclusion on the first send versus subsequent resends.
- Verify that editing an email address does not orphan or invalidate existing credentials.
- Identify other email triggers (password reset, welcome email) that might be impacted by a shared template change.

### Implementation
- [Tag: Sequential — after audit] Modify the resend service to always attach credentials when building the email payload.
- Update the email template if necessary to render the credentials section unconditionally for admin resends.
- Add or update a log entry so admins can confirm that credentials were included in the resent message.

### Validation
- Test with an uncorrected email to confirm credentials are now present.
- Test with a typo-corrected email to confirm credentials are present and the new address receives the full message.
- Regression-test the standard verification-only flows to ensure they are unchanged.

# Objective
## Investigate and Resolve Post-Submission Answer Mismatch Bug and Adjust Score Threshold

---

## Role
You are a software quality assurance engineer and developer. Approach this objective with a diagnostic, detail-oriented, and user-centric mindset.

---

## Description
Investigate a reported issue where some students experience a mismatch between their selected answer and the answer displayed after submission. Other students have not reported this issue, so the investigation must consider that the behavior is inconsistent across users. The goal is to determine whether a bug, logical error, or red flag exists in the submission flow and to apply a reliable fix. Concurrently, adjust the scoring threshold so that students see a passing status when their score is 70% or above, lowering it from the current 75%. The fix and threshold change must be implemented in a way that preserves a fair and consistent experience for all students, including those who have not encountered the mismatch.

---

## Primary Objective
Diagnose and resolve the inconsistent post-submission answer mismatch bug while lowering the passing score threshold to 70%.

---

## Secondary Objectives
- Ensure the fix does not negatively impact students who have never experienced the mismatch.
- Update the passing score display logic so that scores of 70% and above are shown as passed.

---

## Success Criteria
- The root cause of the answer mismatch is identified and corrected.
- No new instances of the answer mismatch occur after the fix is deployed.
- Scores of 70% or higher consistently display a passing status for all students.
- Previously unaffected students continue to see correct answer matches and accurate score displays.

---

## Constraints
- The existing scoring threshold logic must be modified from 75% to 70%.
- Solutions must not introduce regressions for students who have not experienced the bug.

---

## Priority Level
- Investigate and fix the answer mismatch bug as a high-priority issue.
- Implement the score threshold change as part of the same update cycle.

---

## Context & Dependencies
- The mismatch occurs only for some students after form submission.
- The current passing score threshold is set at 75% and must be lowered to 70%.
- The investigation must account for the inconsistent reproduction of the bug.

---

## Stakeholders
- Students who submit answers and review their scores.

---

## Supporting Tasks

- [Tag: Sequential] Reproduce the reported answer mismatch using test cases derived from affected student accounts.
- [Tag: Sequential] Inspect the submission processing pipeline, comparing stored answers with displayed results.
- [Tag: Sequential] Identify any red flags such as race conditions, incorrect database references, or front-end rendering issues.

### Fix Phase
- [Tag: Conditional] Apply a code fix that ensures the submitted answer is correctly retrieved and displayed.
- [Tag: Conditional] Verify the fix does not alter behavior for unaffected users through regression testing.

### Threshold Adjustment Phase
- [Tag: Parallel] Locate the score threshold configuration or logic responsible for pass/fail display.
- [Tag: Parallel] Update the threshold value from 75% to 70% and confirm the change propagates correctly.

### Validation Phase
- [Tag: Sequential] Conduct end-to-end tests covering both affected and unaffected student scenarios.
- [Tag: Sequential] Confirm that scores at exactly 70% display as passed.

## Detailed Breakdown

### Answer Mismatch Investigation
Conduct a thorough examination of the submission and result-display pipeline. Trace the flow from the student's selected answer through storage, retrieval, and final rendering. Look for points where the submitted value could be overwritten, incorrectly mapped, or stale. Consider asynchronous operations that may not complete in the expected order. Since the issue affects only some students, evaluate factors like account state, session data, or timing that could cause the bug to manifest intermittently. Confirm findings with reproducible test cases before proceeding to a fix.

#### Specific Considerations
- Check for client-side caching that might show a different answer than what was submitted.
- Examine database queries to ensure the correct answer record is fetched for each student.
- Validate that no middleware transforms or defaults the answer value after submission.

### Score Threshold Adjustment
Identify all components that determine the passing status, including server-side logic, client-side display conditions, and any shared configuration files. Change the threshold value to 70% and ensure that boundary conditions are handled correctly—specifically, that exactly 70.0% is treated as passing. Update any associated labels, progress bars, or notifications to reflect the new threshold consistently. Test with a range of scores above, at, and below 70% to confirm correct behavior.

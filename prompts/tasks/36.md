# Objective
## Qyzen Terms of Service and Privacy Policy

---

## Description
This document establishes the binding terms of service and the privacy policy for the Qyzen academic assessment and classroom management platform, operated by Mr. Mark Adrianne Salunga. The terms define user eligibility, authentication methods, acceptable use, academic integrity monitoring, content ownership, service availability, account management, and contact procedures. The privacy policy details what personal and academic data is collected, how assessments are monitored for integrity, the purposes of data processing, data visibility across user roles, the third-party service providers involved, and the retention and deletion practices. Together, these documents govern the relationship between the platform operator, institutional users, and individual account holders. Resources (docs\TOS_PP.md)

---

## Primary Objective
Define the legally binding terms of use and data handling practices for all users of the Qyzen platform.

---

## Secondary Objectives
- Establish that account access is institutionally controlled, with no public self-registration.
- Specify the acceptable use policy and prohibited activities.
- Explain the platform’s academic integrity monitoring mechanisms and their limited purpose.
- Clarify data ownership, visibility by role, and third-party infrastructure dependencies.
- Provide contact channels for inquiries and data requests.

---

## Constraints
- Accounts are created and assigned exclusively by an institutional administrator; there is no self-service registration.
- The platform does not use third-party analytics or advertising services.
- Data collected for academic integrity monitoring is used solely for that purpose.

---

## Out of Scope
- Public, self-service account creation.
- Use of user data for advertising or sale to third parties.
- Guaranteed uninterrupted platform availability.

---

## Context & Dependencies
- The platform relies on Supabase for database hosting, authentication, file storage, and real-time functionality.
- Google authentication is an optional sign-in method that shares the user’s Google account email.
- Profile pictures and cover photos are stored in a publicly accessible storage location via direct link, while learning materials are stored in access-controlled storage requiring authentication.

---

## Stated Assumptions
- Continued use of the platform after terms are updated constitutes acceptance of the revised terms.

---

## Stakeholders
- **Mr. Mark Adrianne Salunga**: Platform operator and data controller.
- **Institutional Administrators**: Responsible for account creation, role assignment, and account deactivation.
- **Educators**: Create assessments, monitor student activity and integrity events, and access class academic data and chats.
- **Students**: Participate in assessments, submit answers, and send chat messages, with activity visible to their educators.

---

## Supporting Tasks

### Terms of Service

- Define Qyzen as an academic platform operated by Mr. Mark Adrianne Salunga, binding users upon sign-in.
- State that accounts are institutionally created and assigned, with no public registration.
- Restrict use to the assigned account and require credential confidentiality.
- Permit sign-in via email and password or a matching Google account; deny access for unregistered Google emails and deactivated accounts.
- Prohibit unauthorized data access, credential sharing, uploading unlawful or harmful material, and interfering with platform operations or integrity features.
- Explain that timed assessments monitor for integrity events, which are recorded and shared with the educator; note that retakes, hints, and answer reviews are educator-controlled.
- Clarify that submitted content remains associated with the user’s account and institution, and is visible to educators and administrators for class management.
- State that uninterrupted availability is not guaranteed, citing maintenance, updates, and third-party infrastructure dependencies.
- Reserve the right for institutions to deactivate or remove accounts at any time, and for the operator to update terms with continued use signifying acceptance.
- Provide contact channels: the institution’s administrator and the operator’s email (markadrianne.salunga@ncst.edu.ph).

### Privacy Policy

- Identify Mr. Mark Adrianne Salunga as the data controller.
- Enumerate collected data: account details, optional profile media, academic records, chat messages, and uploaded file metadata.
- Detail assessment monitoring: online status, current page, periodic "last seen" timestamps (every ~25 seconds), and quiz-time integrity events (tab switching, leaving the window, mouse-off-page, copy/paste, right-click, developer tools, screenshots, split-screen resizing).
- State that integrity events are shared with the educator and counted as warnings, used exclusively for academic integrity.
- Define data usage purposes: role-based access, assessment execution and grading, communication, material sharing, and integrity maintenance; explicitly exclude sale or advertising use.
- Define data visibility: users see their own data; educators see class academic data, assessment activity, and chats; administrators manage accounts and system-wide academic data.
- Disclose that profile pictures and cover photos are stored publicly by direct link, while learning materials require authenticated access.
- Identify Supabase as the host for database, authentication, file storage, and real-time features, and Google as an optional authentication provider sharing email only.
- State the absence of third-party analytics or ad tracking.
- Describe data retention aligned with active accounts and institutional recordkeeping needs, with deletion or marking upon account removal.
- Provide contact for data access, correction, or deletion requests: institution administrator or markadrianne.salunga@ncst.edu.ph.
- Reserve the right to update the policy with the updated date reflecting the current version.
